ACCURACY ISSUES: ffxiv_tank_duty_cheatsheet.md
Audit date: 2026-06-18
Severity: CRITICAL

Conclusion
----------
This is not a reliable duty guide. The problem is systemic: many encounter
descriptions are attached to the wrong boss or dungeon, and many mechanics are
invented. Generic advice such as using mitigation and facing enemies away is
often fine, but encounter-specific statements should be independently checked.

Universal tank-system errors
----------------------------

1. "Provoke: Forces max enmity"
   Incomplete/misleading. Current Provoke places the user at the top of the
   target's enmity list and grants additional enmity. It does not create some
   permanent or uncapped "maximum enmity" state.

2. "Shirk is not optional"
   Overstated for this guide's stated normal-duty scope. Shirk is useful for
   planned swaps, but most normal trials and raids do not require tank swaps.

3. "Low Blow: 5-second stun"
   The duration is target-dependent because repeated stuns build resistance.
   Presenting every use as a guaranteed five-second stun is inaccurate.

ACCURACY ISSUES: ffxiv_healer_duty_cheatsheet.md
Audit date: 2026-06-18

Conclusion
----------
The encounter tables inherit the same extensive boss-roster and mechanic errors
documented in ffxiv_tank_duty_cheatsheet_accuracy_issues.txt. This file also
contains major errors about core healer systems and White Mage abilities.

Core healer-system errors
-------------------------

1. "Raised player has Weakness (~100s increased damage taken)"
   False. Weakness reduces Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, and Mind by 25%
   for 100 seconds. It does not increase damage taken. A second raise while
   Weakness is active applies Brink of Death, reducing those attributes by 50%.

2. "Someone dies: Swiftcast + Raise immediately. Now."
   Unsafe as an absolute rule. The healer must first ensure they will survive,
   the tank will survive, and no imminent mechanic will interrupt or kill them.
   Raise timing is situational.

3. Priority list puts tank survival above healer survival.
   A dead healer cannot keep the tank alive or recover the party. In ordinary
   four-player content, self-survival must take priority.

4. "Before a boss: Regen the tank, apply Medica II regen"
   Medica II before every boss is unnecessary, consumes MP and a GCD, and often
   expires without healing meaningful damage. This is not sound general advice.

5. "Plenary Indulgence: use right after an AoE heal ... for bonus healing."
   Backwards. Plenary Indulgence grants Confession first; qualifying AoE heals
   cast while Confession is active trigger the extra healing.

6. "Liturgy of the Bell ... the bell heals when it gets hit ... needs to be
   attacked."
   False. A stack is consumed and healing occurs when the White Mage who placed
   the bell takes damage. Enemies do not attack the bell.

7. "Thin Air ... Otherwise, early in a fight to restore resources."
   False. Thin Air makes the next spell cost no MP. It does not restore MP or
   any other resource.

8. "Cure III: AoE heal requires the party to be stacked"
   Misleading. Cure III is a targeted AoE centered on the selected target. The
   party must be within its radius of that target, not necessarily stacked on
   the White Mage.

9. "Temperance: 10% damage reduction for the party"
   Imprecise. Temperance reduces damage taken by self and nearby party members
   and increases the White Mage's healing magic potency. Range and timing
   matter; it is not an unrestricted party-wide effect.

10. "Paralysis — Esuna immediately. A paralyzed tank is a dead tank."
    Hyperbolic and often false. Cleansing it is useful, but most normal-content
    paralysis effects do not automatically kill a tank.

- Qarn Doom is on Teratotaur, not Adjudicator.
- Forgall is not an Amdapor Keep boss.
- Siren is not in Pharos Sirius (Hard).
- Nidhogg is not in Sohr Khai.

- Ghimlyt Dark has no ceruleum-tank duty-action boss phase.
- Holminster's actual bosses are Forgiven Dissonance, Tesleen, and Philia.
- Amaurot has no Proto-Ultima orb-collision encounter.
- Mt. Gulg does not have the repeated Gibbet Cage claims in this guide.
- Dead Ends has no Ma Famfrit/Breathtaker air-bubble encounter.

- Smileton bosses are real combat encounters, not healing-free puzzles.

Additional unsafe generalizations
---------------------------------

- Repeatedly calling routine normal-dungeon hits "oGCD mandatory" is false.
- Repeatedly prescribing Temperance for ordinary boss casts ignores level sync;

- The checklist says "Regen on tank before first pull" as an unconditional

- "Do not chain GCD heals on trash; one Regen and watch" can kill an

Core DPS-system errors
----------------------

1. "If you pull aggro ... use your enmity drop if available"
   Obsolete. Current PvE DPS jobs do not have the old general enmity-reduction
   actions such as Diversion, Lucid Dreaming's former enmity effect, or
   Tactician's former enmity effect.

2. "Wait ... Two GCDs is usually enough"
   Outdated and unnecessary as a general rule. With tank stance active, one
   connected tank AoE normally establishes overwhelming pack enmity. Waiting
   two full GCDs reduces damage and does not fix a tank who missed a target.

3. "Raise (RDM / SMN / BRD / MCH / DNC)"
   False. Red Mage and Summoner can raise in normal PvE. Bard, Machinist, and
   Dancer cannot.

4. "RDM, SMN, BRD, MCH, DNC have access to Raise or equivalent."
   False for BRD, MCH, and DNC. Their PvE kits contain no Raise equivalent.

5. "Stun (Low Blow equivalent, job-specific)"
   Low Blow is a tank role action. Melee DPS have Leg Sweep. Physical ranged
   and magical ranged jobs do not all have a generic equivalent stun.

6. "Feint ... for the whole party on one hit"
   False description. Feint debuffs one enemy for a duration, reducing its
   physical damage more strongly and magical damage less strongly. It is not a
   one-hit party buff.

7. "Addle ... magic damage reduction for the whole party"
   False description. Addle debuffs one enemy for a duration, reducing its
   magical damage more strongly and physical damage less strongly.

8. "Towers ... A dead tower is a wipe."
   Not generally true in normal duties. An unsoaked tower may deal damage,
   apply a vulnerability, kill players, or wipe depending on the encounter.

9. Positionals section says "The tail is rear. Stay out of tail swipes."
   Bosses without a complete rear positional ring may not have meaningful
   rear/flank requirements, and modern bosses rarely use an untelegraphed
   generic tail swipe. This is not a reliable universal rule.

10. "Physical ranged ... baiting arena AoEs away from the party"
    Not a universal responsibility. Baits are encounter-specific; arbitrary
    ranged positioning can spread players, disrupt healing, or bait mechanics
    into bad locations.

- Qarn Doom is on Teratotaur, not Adjudicator.

- Amdapor Keep's final boss is Anantaboga, not Forgall.

- Kugane Castle has no real-copy Tengu boss or Gilgamesh final boss.

Other inaccurate job advice
---------------------------

- "Swiftcast: Best uses are movement mechanics and raises (RDM / SMN)."
  Summoner's Swiftcast usage is rotation- and situation-dependent; Red Mage can
  use Dualcast to raise and does not generally need Swiftcast for every raise.

- "BLM: your movement is the most restricted."
  Too absolute. Modern Black Mage has multiple movement tools; mobility depends

- "True North doesn't help [BLM]."
  Technically true because BLM has no positionals, but presenting it as a
  movement comparison is category confusion: True North never grants movement.

ACCURACY ISSUES: ffxiv_squadron_notes.md
Audit date: 2026-06-18
Severity: LOW

Verified accurate
-----------------

- One member at level 20 unlocks Flagged Mission: Voidsent Elimination.
- Completing it unlocks Routine Missions.
- One member at level 40 unlocks Flagged Mission: Crystal Recovery.
- Completing it unlocks Priority Missions and First Lieutenant.
- Five unique Command Mission dungeons plus Crystal Recovery unlock Sapper
  Strike; completing Sapper Strike unlocks Captain.
- Squadron missions take 18 hours.
- Failed missions give half base XP.
- Flagged missions fail if any required stat is unmet.
- Class-change books become purchasable at First Lieutenant.
- Changing a recruit's class lowers their level by one.
- Defense/Offense/Magicks book role groupings are correct.

Issue requiring qualification
-----------------------------

1. "Ungarmax ... gives party damage up"
   Ungarmax historically dealt damage and applied a party damage buff, but it
   has been adjusted over the game's life and is a squadron-only action. The
   exact potency/buff value and whether it remains the optimal use should be
   checked in-game rather than treated as a timeless fact. The guide provides
   no current Patch 7.5 source for this detail.

Opinion presented too strongly
