# FFXIV Notes: Sirclucks XP + Market Board Farming

This is a working note dump for farming monster drops, leveling Sirclucks, and turning lazy crafter behavior into gil.

## Core idea

Use overworld farming to do three things at once:

1. Level Sirclucks.
2. Farm monster drops that crafters buy.
3. Sell those drops in stack sizes that make sense for crafting recipes.

The ideal farm is not a rare jackpot item. It is boring crafting material that people need repeatedly and do not want to farm themselves.

---

## Sirclucks / companion XP loop

### Curiel Root basics

Curiel Roots have two separate uses:

- **Stable training:** Feed Curiel Root as the training reward at the chocobo stable.
- **Field feeding:** Summon Sirclucks with Gysahl Greens, then feed Curiel Root while he is out.

### Favorite food setup

To make Curiel Root Sirclucks's favorite food:

1. Stable Sirclucks.
2. Train him.
3. Use **Curiel Root** as the reward.
4. Repeat until Curiel has been used for stable training **10 times**.

After that, Curiel Root becomes his favorite food. Once it is his favorite, using Curiel Root in the field gives the better companion EXP buff.

Important: The one-hour stable timer is only the training cooldown. It is **not** a bird jail sentence. You can train him, then fetch him immediately and keep playing.

### Practical routine

During play:

1. Fetch Sirclucks from the stable.
2. Summon him with Gysahl Greens.
3. Feed Curiel Root only when doing overworld killing/FATEs/hunts/farms.
4. Keep him summoned by refreshing greens before the timer expires.

When logging off:

1. Stable Sirclucks.
2. Train with Curiel Root if the cooldown is ready.
3. Leave him stabled while offline.

### What wastes Curiel Root field value

The field buff is useful while Sirclucks stays summoned. Do not burn Curiel Roots during heavy cutscene/city/duty time.

Things that kill the value:

- Sirclucks times out.
- Sirclucks is withdrawn/dismissed.
- Sirclucks dies.
- You log out.
- You go into duties where he cannot accompany you.

## Market Board rules

### Buying

Always check both:

- **Unit price**
- **Total stack price / quantity**

Classic trap:

> Unit price is great, but the stack has 109 items and you meant to buy 10.

That is not necessarily a scam. It is just bulk purchasing by accident.

### Selling

There are multiple buyer types:

- **Convenience buyers:** Want 5, 10, 20, 21, 30. They pay more per unit because they do not want a warehouse.
- **Normal buyers:** Want enough to craft a sane batch.
- **Bulk buyers:** Want 99, 500, 999 and expect a lower unit price.
- **Sleep-deprived market victims:** See good unit price, miss the quantity, and buy the whole damn thing.

Legit seller play:

- Put up small/medium stacks at a convenience premium.
- Put up one big bulk stack at a lower unit price.
- Avoid weird leftover stack sizes unless dumping inventory.

### Sale history beats listing price

Listing price tells you what sellers want.

Sale history tells you what buyers actually paid.

Before farming for 30+ minutes:

1. Search the item on your Market Board.
2. Open sale history.
3. Check whether it is actually moving.
4. Check stack sizes that recently sold.
5. Match your listing sizes to what buyers are already buying.

Universalis is useful for scouting, but your own world's Market Board sale history is the source of truth.

## Stack sizing logic

If a recipe uses **3 skins per leather**, sell stacks divisible by 3.

Good stack sizes:

| Stack | Crafting meaning |
|---:|---|
| 9 | 3 leathers |
| 12 | 4 leathers |
| 15 | 5 leathers |
| 21 | 7 leathers |
| 30 | 10 leathers |
| 60 | 20 leathers |
| 99 | 33 leathers |

**21** is a strong convenience stack: big enough to matter, small enough that the buyer is not committing to bird Costco levels of inventory.

Suggested listing pattern:

- A few stacks of **21** at convenience pricing.
- One stack of **60** or **99** at a slightly better unit price.
- Keep true bulk stacks only for items with strong movement.

## Candidate Heavensward monster-drop farms

These are not guaranteed money. They are candidate farms. Check your server first.

### 1. Archaeornis Skin

**Zone:** Coerthas Western Highlands  
**Mob:** Archaeornis / Grand Archaeornis  
**Why it matters:** Used for Archaeornis Leather. Leather-type materials often move because crafters burn through them.

Suggested stacks:

- 21
- 30
- 60
- 99

Notes:

- Early Heavensward candidate.
- Good first farm once Coerthas Western Highlands opens up.
- Also available through hunting retainer ventures once your retainer level catches up.

### 2. Deepeye Tears

**Zone:** Coerthas Western Highlands  
**Mob:** Deepeye / Bloodshot Deepeye / The Beholder  
**Why it matters:** Reagent material. Reagents can be good, but price movement is server-dependent.

Suggested stacks:

- 10
- 20
- 50
- 99

Notes:

- Check sale history carefully. Some reagent mats list high but move slowly.
- Nice early HW target because the mobs are around level 50.

### 3. Wyvern Skin

**Zones:** The Dravanian Forelands / The Churning Mists  
**Mobs:** Dravanian Wyvern, Cloud Wyvern, Elder Wyvern, Starkshadow Wyvern  
**Why it matters:** Used for Wyvern Leather. Another 3-skins-per-leather style material.

Suggested stacks:

- 21
- 30
- 60
- 99

Notes:

- Very on-brand farm: kill dragon-adjacent assholes with Sirclucks.
- Check sale history for both Wyvern Skin and Wyvern Wing. If both move, this farm gets better.

### 4. Dhalmel Hide

**Zone:** The Sea of Clouds  
**Mob:** Dhalmel  
**Why it matters:** Used for Dhalmel Leather.

Suggested stacks:

- 21
- 30
- 60
- 99

Notes:

- The "slaughter giraffe dinosaurs for capitalism" option.
- Check whether Dhalmel Leather itself sells better than the hides. If the crafted leather moves faster and you can craft it later, that may be higher profit.

### 5. Amphiptere Skin

**Zones:** The Churning Mists / Azys Lla / some Coerthas Western Highlands sources  
**Mobs:** Amphiptere-type mobs  
**Why it matters:** Used for Amphiptere Leather.

Suggested stacks:

- 21
- 30
- 60
- 99

Notes:

- Later Heavensward candidate.
- Check current MB history before committing. Later-zone mats can be weird: sometimes valuable, sometimes dead.

## Actual farming workflow

1. Pick one candidate item.
2. Check Market Board sale history.
3. Check current listings by unit price and stack size.
4. Farm for 15–20 minutes with Sirclucks summoned.
5. Track:
   - drop count
   - gil value
   - time spent
   - Sirclucks XP gained
6. List in intentional stacks.
7. Recheck the next day and adjust.

Simple decision rule:

- If it sells within a day or two: keep farming.
- If it sits: lower price, split stacks differently, or move on.
- If the unit price is good but sale history is dead: trap item; do not marry it.

## Retainer angle

Once your WAR retainer levels high enough:

- Send them on hunting ventures for the same kind of monster drops.
- Use your old WAR/tank gear as retainer gear.
- Farming + retainer ventures can feed the same Market Board niche.

Retainer strategy:

- Player farms while Sirclucks levels.
- Retainer farms while you do MSQ/duties/log off.
- Market Board listings stay up while retainers are on ventures.
