# FFXIV Tank Duty Cheatsheet

Practical tank notes for normal queued PvE duties.

**Scope**

This file is meant for normal/MSQ/roulette tanking, especially Warrior. It covers:

- Dungeons
- Normal / Hard trials
- Alliance raids
- Normal raids
- High-level “instance families” like Deep Dungeon / Variant / Criterion at a practical level

It does **not** try to be a full EX/Savage/Ultimate guide. For those, use current PF macros, toolboxes, and video guides. Mechanics and strats change by party culture, patch power creep, and cheese.

## Universal tank rules

### Trash pulls

```text
Tank stance on.
Pull with ranged attack or gap closer.
AoE everything.
Stop moving once the pack is gathered.
Face mobs away from party.
Use mitigation on trash, not only bosses.
```

Tank stances by job: WAR = Defiance, PLD = Iron Will, DRK = Grit, GNB = Royal Guard.

Trash usually hurts more than bosses.

Good mitigation rhythm:

```text
Pack 1: Rampart
Pack 2: Vengeance / job mit
Pack 3: Reprisal + Arm's Length
Emergency: Holmgang / Equilibrium / Bloodwhetting / potion / prayer to Sirclucks
```

Do not press every cooldown at once unless the pull is actively becoming a crime scene.

### Bosses

```text
Face boss away from party.
Keep boss still unless mechanics force movement.
Use short mitigation on tankbusters.
Use Reprisal on raidwides.
Pick up adds immediately.
If the other tank has aggro and seems competent, stay OT unless a swap/add mechanic happens.
```

### Tank utility abilities

```text
Provoke: Forces max enmity on your current target. Use to grab a loose mob or initiate a tank swap.
Shirk: Transfers 25% of your enmity to a targeted party member. Aim at the MT after a swap.
Standard tank swap: OT provokes when MT needs the swap → MT shirks to OT immediately after.
Interject: Interrupts a silver/sparkle cast bar. Use it on trash. Some older bosses are interruptible too.
Low Blow: 5-second stun. Great for pausing a scary trash cast or buying a GCD of breathing room.
```

Shirk is not optional. If you are tanking 8-player content and not using Shirk on swaps, your co-tank
is working harder than they should be. Establish the habit now.

### Normal trial / raid tank etiquette

```text
If you pull first, you are MT.
If the other tank pulls first, you are OT.
Do not provoke-war unless the MT is dead, cleaving party, or clearly lost.
If adds spawn, OT usually grabs them.
If tanks get separate markers, separate.
If tanks get shared stack markers, stack together unless the fight clearly says otherwise.
```

### Alliance raid tank etiquette

```text
Your alliance = your party.
A tank usually takes their alliance's add.
For bosses, let one tank MT unless mechanics require three tanks.
Do not spin the boss because your ego needs aggro.
If another alliance tank is already stable, just be useful.
```

### Common mechanic language

| Mechanic | Tank answer |
|---|---|
| Tankbuster | Use mitigation. Usually does not require a swap in normal mode. |
| Shared tankbuster | Stack with other tank. Mitigate. |
| Double tankbuster | Both tanks may be hit. Spread if marked separately. |
| Raidwide | Reprisal if available. |
| Add spawn | Pick it up fast. |
| Tether to tank/add | Separate or drag as indicated. |
| Boss jumps away | Do not panic. Find boss, re-center if needed. |
| Doom | Usually cleanse, heal to full, stand on pad, or kill add. Watch what party does. |
| Look-away eye | Face camera/character away. |
| Knockback | Arm's Length/Surecast if allowed, otherwise position. |
| Forced march / confusion | Stop trying to greed. Read arrow/status. |
| Towers | Stand in them if no one else does; tanks are good tower insurance. |
| Stack marker (party/light party) | Get in the stack. Tanks soak fine with party. |
| Spread marker | Separate from everyone. Do not share your marker. |
| Vuln up (stacking) | You take more damage per stack. Swap if it climbs. Use invuln only if the stack will kill you before a swap is possible. |
| Enrage | Boss kill-cast. If it completes, the run ends. Call it out if DPS looks slow. |

## Current-story fast lane: Heavensward, around The Limitless Blue / The Vault

You are around late base HW. These are the relevant near-term duties.

### The Limitless Blue (Hard)

- Do **not** tank Bismarck like a normal boss. The boss is mostly arena/setpiece.
- Tank adds cleanly.
- When two big serpent adds spawn, tanks separate them.
- Hit Bismarck's exposed weak point when the party chains/pulls him in.
- Watch platform mechanics. If everyone moves to devices or away from an edge, follow the herd.
- This is sky-whaling OSHA paperwork, not a pure tank check.

### The Vault

- Pull cleanly; trash can be spicy if overpulled.
- Face bosses away.
- Watch for line/charge mechanics and party stack/spread behavior.
- Use mitigation on boss casts that look tankbuster-ish.
- The main tank job is stability. The dungeon itself is there to Final Fantasy you emotionally.

### The Aery

- Dragon trash, obvious AoEs, and boss mechanics are the deal.
- Face bosses away from party.
- Pick up adds.
- Do not get cute while sleep-deprived. WAR self-healing is not a medical device.

### ARR dungeon gotchas

**Copperbell Mines** — Party must interact with the prisoner cages at set points to progress boss phases. Cannot be DPS-skipped.

**Brayflox's Longstop** — Hellbender (first boss) constantly drops poison puddles under itself. Drag it out of them; standing in the puddles kills the party faster than the boss does.

**The Sunken Temple of Qarn** — Doom on the Adjudicator fight: stand on the glowing stone floor slab before the timer hits zero. The slab is in the center of the room. Missing it is instant death regardless of HP.

**Cutter's Cry** — Chimera: Dragon's Voice = donut AoE, get in close to the boss. Ram's Voice = point-blank AoE, get away from the boss. Everyone reverses these on the first run.

**Dzemael Darkhold** — The crystal pillars in the Batraal boss room reduce incoming damage significantly. Pull and keep the boss near a pillar. Fighting outside the light makes it much more painful than it needs to be.

**The Aurum Vale** — Morbol boss stacks a poison debuff that kills you without a cure. The gleaming rinds on the ground (Ovibos Milk) cleanse it; pick them up and use them. Also: pull the first room in small groups. Overpulling the entry corridor is the most common disaster in this dungeon.

**The Wanderer's Palace** — Tonberry King accumulates resentment from every hit it lands on any party member. At high stacks it one-shots whoever it looks at. Kill it fast.

**Amdapor Keep** — Demon Wall pushes forward and knocks you into the pit behind the party. Move toward the boss continuously or the wall wins.

### HW dungeon gotchas

**Neverreap** — Khimaira (first boss) uses a knockback near the arena edge. Use Arm's Length or stay toward the center. Getting knocked off the floating platform is a long run back.

**The Vault** — Ser Charibert (second boss) lights candles that spread fire across the floor. Move the boss around the room as fire claims floor space. Tanks who plant in one spot end up fighting in a fire pit.

**The Great Gubal Library** — Evisiscarae (second boss): avoid stepping on the dark open-book tiles on the floor. They deal heavy damage. Navigate around them while keeping the boss faced away.

**The Antitower** — Calcabrina (final boss) spawns golden and silver chess piece adds. Keep them separated. If they converge they enrage/buff each other and the fight deteriorates fast.

**Sohr Khai** — One encounter has a dragon-eye gaze attack. Turn your character and camera away when the eye activates. The tell is a red glow; looking at it applies a crippling debuff.

**Baelsar's Wall** — Magitek Ray enemies in trash pulls have frontal line AoEs. They read as normal trash but will cleave the party if you let them face the wrong way.

# Variant / Criterion / Deep Dungeon / Field Operation notes

These are “instances,” but they do not fit normal roulette tanking.

## Variant dungeons

- Flexible party size/roles.
- If you tank, play like a normal dungeon but expect route mechanics.
- Use self-sustain and interrupts.
- Bosses often have route-dependent mechanics.

## Criterion dungeons

- Harder 4-player content.
- Treat trash like mini-raid pulls.
- Use planned mitigation.
- Interrupt/stun responsibilities matter.
- Do not blind-prog as “just a dungeon” unless everyone agreed.

## Deep Dungeons

Examples: Palace of the Dead, Heaven-on-High, Eureka Orthos, Pilgrim's Traverse.

Tank notes:

- Your normal gear mostly does not matter; the system gear matters.
- Pull carefully.
- Do not face-pull extra rooms.
- Respect patrols.
- Bosses are usually mechanic checks more than tank checks.
- Sustain is useful, but overconfidence gets people deleted.

## Field operations / large-scale instances

Examples: Eureka, Bozja/Zadnor, large-scale critical engagements.

Tank notes:

- Your job is often add control, boss stability, and survival.
- Use duty/system actions correctly.
- Watch shout chat / markers.
- Do not assume normal dungeon rules apply.

# Warrior-specific notes

## Low / mid level

- Overpower/Mythril Tempest for packs.
- Keep Storm's Eye buff up once available.
- Use Raw Intuition/Bloodwhetting like the beautiful nonsense button it is.
- Equilibrium is not a decoration.
- Holmgang before death, not after.

## Mid / high level additions

- **Combo path:** Storm's Path generates Beast Gauge and procs Bloodwhetting healing. Storm's Eye keeps your damage buff up. In practice: maintain Storm's Eye, then Path to build gauge. Do not let Storm's Eye fall off.
- **Inner Release (level 70):** DPS burst cooldown. Press it on cooldown during a damage window, not reactively as panic mitigation. All GCDs inside the window should be free. Do not waste it.
- **Nascent Flash (level 76):** Applies a heal-over-time to a party member and feeds your own Bloodwhetting healing off the next few hits. Aim it at a healer who is getting pressured, not just yourself.
- **Shake It Off:** Party shield. Use it before a raidwide hits, not after. Stack it with Thrill of Battle for a bigger shield.

## Pull sizing

```text
New dungeon / blind / tired:
one pack or modest pulls

Healer strong / party blasting:
two packs

Wall-to-wall:
only if gear, healer, and dungeon layout support it
```

## Defensive rhythm

```text
Before damage lands:
Rampart / Vengeance / Bloodwhetting

During damage:
Equilibrium / Thrill / Shake / Reprisal

When it is going to shit:
Holmgang
```

## The actual tank fantasy

Your job is not to be fancy.

Your job is to make the run boring for everyone else.

Boring tanking = good tanking.
