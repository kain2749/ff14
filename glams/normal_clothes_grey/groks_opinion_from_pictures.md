# FF14 Glam Review: Casual Urban Warrior

**I really like this glam!** It's got a super strong, cohesive "modern urban warrior" vibe that feels fresh and characterful in FFXIV.

### First impressions from the visuals:
- **Casual but intentional**: The grey t-shirt + blue jeans + beanie combo gives off strong "off-duty adventurer who just rolled out of a coffee shop in Gridania" energy. It feels lived-in and practical rather than trying too hard to look "fantasy." The beanie and choker add a bit of edge and personality without being over-the-top.
- **Color story**: The muted greys and blues keep it grounded and cool-toned, while the bright pink/purple hair and that little green choker jewel provide nice pop. It makes her stand out without clashing with the environment.
- **The axe is the star**: That big greataxe (especially in the action pose) completely sells the contrast. She looks like someone who could be sipping a drink one minute and cleaving a primal in half the next. The way it's slung on her back or held ready shows she's *ready*.

### What the appearance tells me about the character:
- **Confident and approachable**: The relaxed stance and everyday clothes say "I'm chill until it's time not to be." She doesn't look like she's trying to intimidate random people, but that axe and battle pose make it very clear she can handle herself (and probably enjoys it).
- **Practical fighter**: This isn't a fancy noble or ceremonial glam — it's functional streetwear. Suggests a Warrior of Light who's been through a lot but keeps things simple and grounded. Maybe a bit of a lone wolf who still cares about looking decent in public.
- **Youthful rebel energy**: The pink hair, beanie, and casual layers give off "I do what I want" vibes. She feels like the type who'd show up to important Scion meetings in this and not give a damn what anyone thinks.
- **A bit mysterious/playful**: The hair color and accessories hint at a more colorful personality underneath the understated outfit. Like there's a story there — maybe she dyes her hair to remember something, or it's just how she expresses herself in a world full of primals and politics.

Overall, it's a really solid, lore-friendly "modern" glam that still screams **Warrior** (Job). It has great silhouette and readability even from a distance, and the contrast between the mundane clothes and the massive axe is chef's kiss.

Would look killer in Limsa or Kugane just hanging out, and terrifying on the battlefield. Nice work — this character feels like she has *presence*.
